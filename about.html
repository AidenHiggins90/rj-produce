Our Story

Built on trust, grown into a supply chain

In 2004, Rick Gorena started RJ Produce out of Pharr, Texas with little more than a handful of grower relationships and a belief that the produce business runs on trust. In a region where most operators chased the lowest price, he bet on something different: showing up, keeping his word, and treating growers and buyers as long-term partners.

Those early relationships became the foundation. As farmers on both sides of the border learned they could count on RJ Produce season after season, the business grew — from local deliveries to supplying branded product to some of the largest retailers in the country, including Costco and Restaurant Depot.

In 2021, that two-decade track record drew the attention of GrubMarket, and RJ Produce joined their family of companies. The partnership brought new technology and national reach — but the way we do business hasn't changed since day one.

Our values

What drives us every day

The values that started this company still run it. We keep the commitments we make, even when it costs us — in this business, a reputation is built handshake by handshake, and lost just as fast. We're rooted in the Rio Grande Valley and invested in the growers and families on both sides of the border who make the work possible. And we hold our facilities, processes, and scheduling to a standard that lets a partner expect the exact same RJ Produce on a quiet Tuesday as on the busiest week of the year.

What sets us apart

The numbers behind the partnership

A few simple facts explain why buyers stay with us. Our calendar never has a gap — one region's harvest picks up as another winds down, so a contract with us holds for a full year. Every box traces to a grower we deal with directly, so pricing reflects the produce, not a chain of brokers. And because our facility carries the certification major retailers require, the same standards apply whether you order a pallet or a truckload.
